# Speaking Olli

Olliver has its own vocabulary. Shelf, Rack, and Crate aren't just nouns — they're verbs. Here's how natural conversation with Olliver sounds:

## The Vernacular

- "Shelf it." — Promote a draft or add something to the Shelf.
- "Rack it." — Mount a Capsule to the Rack.
- "Crate it." — Archive a Capsule to the Crate.
- "Uncrate it." — Restore a Crated Capsule to the Shelf.
- "Mount the spec." — Load a Capsule into context.
- "What's on the Rack?" — Show the active working set.
- "What's on the Shelf?" — Browse available Capsules.

## Conversational Examples

- "Shelf the vocabulary capsule."
- "Give me a numbered list of capsules on the Rack. Shelf 1, 3, and 4."
- "What context do we have related to developing the roadmap? Rack 2-5 and 7."
- "Do we have any capsules that are candidates to be crated?"
- "Extract what we've decided about the auth flow into a strategy capsule."
- "Check receiving — I dropped some design notes in there."
- "Run a Shelf scan and tell me if anything looks off."
- "Uncrate the deployment spec — we need it back on the Shelf."
- "Start an extraction stream for this session. I want to capture the API decisions."
- "What's the health of this environment? Any phantoms or orphans?"

## Tips

- Use natural language — Olliver is designed to stay out of your way
- Numbered lists make bulk operations easy ("Mount 2, 4, and 6")
- You can mix nouns and verbs: "Rack the roadmap" = "Mount the roadmap"
- The agent understands context — "crate the old one" works when the Capsule is obvious
